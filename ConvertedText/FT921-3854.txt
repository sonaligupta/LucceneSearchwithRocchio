FT  12 MAR 92 / International Capital Markets: China to open third stock
market


   By REUTER


   BEIJING, TAIPEI


CHINA has announced plans to open a third stock market and attract more
foreign investment in shares. The move will mark a further victory for
economic reformers, led by supreme leader Deng Xiaoping, Reuter reports from
Beijing.
The New China News Agency (NCNA) quoted an official with the People's Bank
of China as saying the exchange would be located somewhere in northern
China. Blueprints would be drawn up this year.
Existing exchanges in Shanghai and the special economic zone of Shenzhen in
southern China would also attract more listings. It is understood the two
markets had been authorised to issue Dollars 400m this year in special 'B'
shares reserved for foreign investors.
Taiwan is to allow its companies to sell their stocks abroad and permit
issues of shares by foreign concerns on the island, the Securities and
Exchange Commission (SEC) said, Reuter reports from Taipei.
