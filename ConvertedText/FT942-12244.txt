FT  30 APR 94 / UK Company News: Grampian TV up 18% to Pounds 3.8m


Grampian Television, the independent broadcaster for north Scotland,
yesterday announced an 18 per cent increase in pre-tax profits to Pounds
3.8m for the year ended February 28 1994.
Turnover was marginally lower at Pounds 20.53m (Pounds 20.62m). The shares
climbed 8p to 267p.
Mr Calum MacLeod, in his first year as chairman, said the result represented
a good start to the group's new licence period both in financial terms and
in regional programme service.
The profit was struck after a complete year of licence payments to Channel 4
of Pounds 722,000 (Pounds 90,000).
Earnings per share rose to 18.71p (15.68p) and the dividend is lifted from
6.1p to 7.5p with a final of 6p.
