FT  16 NOV 93 / People: British Soft Drinks Association


Robert Hayward, former Tory MP for Kingswood, far right, has been appointed
director general of the British Soft Drinks Association, which represents
the UK's Pounds 6bn-a-year industry, the fastest growing sector of the
grocery trade.
An economics graduate, Hayward, 44, was a personnel and industrial relations
manager, as well as a member of Coventry council, before he entered the
Commons in 1983.
During his nine years at Westminster, Hayward served as parliamentary
private secretary to a number of ministers.
Hayward was awarded the OBE for his work as a co-founder of the Gulf Support
Group. The group represented families and friends of Iraqi hostages.
