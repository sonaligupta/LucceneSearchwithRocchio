FT  27 SEP 94 / World Trade News: Taiwanese chip plant prepares expansion


   By LAURA TYSON


   TAIPEI


Texas Instruments-Acer, a Taiwan-based semiconductor manufacturer, has
announced a Dollars 400m expansion project to produce advanced computer
memory chips, writes Laura Tyson in Taipei.
The company has signed a Dollars 210m syndicated loan with 15 foreign and
two domestic banks. The new plant will make 16-megabit dynamic random access
memory chips on eight-inch wafers. Taiwan's only DRAM producer, TI-Acer
currently produces 4-megabit chips on six-inch wafers. Chips will be made
using 0.5 and 0.35-micron process technology. Acer holds a 58 per cent stake
in TI-Acer, Texas Instruments has a 26 per cent share and China Development
Corporation, controlled by Taiwan's ruling Kuomintang, owns 16 per cent.
