FT  24 AUG 92 / Party aide 'given Y500m'


   By REUTER


   TOKYO


A secretary to an influential ruling party politician is reported to have
accepted hundreds of millions of yen from a transport company at the heart
of Japan's latest funding scandal, Reuter reports from Tokyo.
A secretary to Mr Shin Kanemaru, vice-president of the Liberal Democratic
party (LDP), received Y500m (Pounds 2m) in cash before the 1989 election
from Mr Hiroyasu Watanabe, former chief of Tokyo Sagawa Kyubin, television
and newspapers said.
The daily Asahi Shimbun said Mr Watanabe, 58, admitted handing the money to
one of Mr Kanemaru's secretaries in June 1989.
