FT  04 AUG 92 / World Commodities Prices: Tea


There was strong general demand with 13,174 packages for the day reports the
Tea Broker's Association. Landed good medium and bright liquoring East
African teas attracted keen competition and were often 10 to 15p dearer.
Plain sorts including most Central Africans met limited enquiry at
irregularly easier rates. Ceylons came to a selective market with brighter
teas fully firm but the remainder tending easier. Offshore improved demand
and brighter teas were generally dearer. The highest price realised this
week was 202p for a Rwanda pf. 1 Quotations: quality 165p nom good medium
140p medium 120p low medium 82p.
