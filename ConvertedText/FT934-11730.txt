FT  29 OCT 93 / International Company News: Tobacco side helps Imasco lift
profit 10%


   By ROBERT GIBBENS


   MONTREAL


IMASCO, the tobacco, fast food and financial services group, which is 40 per
cent owned by BAT Industries of Britain, achieved a 10 per cent gain in
profit for the third quarter. All operating units are now showing stronger
results.
Net profit was CDollars 122.2m, (USDollars 91.10m) or Dollars 1 a share,
against Dollars 111.5m, or 88 cents, a year earlier, on net revenues of
Dollars 2bn, little changed.
Imperial Tobacco provided the biggest share of operating income, Dollars
127m, against Dollars 119m, followed by CT Financial Services, Dollars 57m,
against Dollars 58m, Hardee's Dollars 42m, against Dollars 33m, Shoppers
Drug Dollars 25m, against Dollars 23m, and Genstar Development Dollars 9m,
against Dollars 6m
Nine months' earnings were Dollars 283.5m, or Dollars 2.24 a share, against
Dollars 269.7m, or Dollars 2.09 a share, up 5 per cent, on net revenues
little changed at Dollars 6bn.
Imperial now have 67.3 per cent of the domestic manufactured cigarette
market. Mr Purdy Crawford, chairman, said Imasco expects to show good growth
for all 1993.
