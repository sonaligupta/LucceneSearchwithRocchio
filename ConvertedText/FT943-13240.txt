FT  20 JUL 94 / International Company News: Blockbuster Entertainment ahead


   By MARTIN DICKSON


   NEW YORK


Blockbuster Entertainment, the US video rental company which is diversifying
into other entertainment businesses, has reported a 31 per cent increase in
net income for the second-quarter of 1994.
The group made Dollars 63m, or 25 cents a share, compared with Dollars 48m,
or 22 cents, in the same period last year, while revenues rose from Dollars
492.5m to Dollars 676.3m. System-wide revenue, which includes sales from
franchised video stores, totalled Dollars 841.4m, up 29 per cent.
Mr Wayne Huizenga, chairman, said same store revenues had risen by 13.1 per
cent at outlets in operation for more than a year, while music retailing
operations had been enhanced by the continuing consolidation of its buying,
distribution and administrative functions.
For the first six months of the year, net income rose by 46 per cent to
Dollars 135.6m, or 53 cents a share, compared with Dollars 92.6m, or 44
cents, in the first half of 1993. Company revenues totalled Dollars 1.37bn,
compared with Dollars 925m.
Mr Huizenga added that the company's programming and distribution businesses
continued to benefit from the domestic and international success of its
television programming and the distribution of its library.
