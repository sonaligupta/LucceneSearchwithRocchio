FT  19 MAY 92 / World Commodities Prices: Tea


There was good but less active demand this week, reports the Tea Brokers
Association. Landed bright liquoring and good medium East Africans proved
irregularly easier by 10 to 20p. Prices for medium descriptions also decline
by 5 to 15p. Plainer teas and a smaller selection of Central Africans were 5
to 10p easier. The few quality Ceylons sold readily but at lower levels
while South Indians met a selective enquiry at easier rates. Offshore
limited demand with prices 5 to 10p lower. The highest price realised this
week was 202p for a rwanda pf. 1 quotations: quality 145p good medium 130p
medium 105p low medium 88p.
