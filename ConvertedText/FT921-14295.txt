FT  16 JAN 92 / UK Company News: London Scottish off 18% after provisions


Provisions against a sharp increase in customers' arrears drove down profits
by 18 per cent at London Scottish Bank, the consumer loans group, in the
year to October 29.
The pre-tax line declined from Pounds 3.8m to Pounds 3.1m. The total bad
debt charge was Pounds 2.1m, made up of provisions of Pounds 1.2m and debt
write-offs of Pounds 900,000.
Turnover fell from Pounds 19.4m to Pounds 19.1m. Earnings per share dipped
from 5.3p to 4.6p fully diluted, but the final dividend is unchanged at
1.875p, leaving the total for the year at 2.75p.
