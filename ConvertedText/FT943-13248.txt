FT  20 JUL 94 / International Company News: Reebok climbs in second term


   By RICHARD TOMKINS


Reebok International, the US sports shoe maker, yesterday reported a
recovery in net income to Dollars 51m in the second quarter from Dollars 41m
last time, writes Richard Tomkins. Sales rose by 18 per cent to Dollars
776.8m and earnings per share, boosted by a heavy stock repurchasing
programme, rose by 30 per cent to 60 cents.
Mr Paul Fireman, chairman and chief executive, acknowledged that Reebok had
lost market share in footwear but highlighted the company's success in
penetrating the market for cleated products used in football, baseball and
soccer.
