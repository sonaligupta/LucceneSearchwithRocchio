FT  01 JUL 92 / UK Company News: New business growth helpslift Lowndes
Lambert


   By RICHARD LAPPER


LOWNDES LAMBERT, the ins-urance broker which was floated last year,
yesterday announced a 26 per cent increase in pre-tax profits for the 12
months to March 31.
Buoyed by a strong growth in new business, pre-tax profits rose to Pounds
9.3m (Pounds 7.4m), pushing earnings per share up 21 per cent to 25.8p.
Lowndes recommended a final dividend of 8.4p, making a total of 12.6p.
The shares closed 3p up at 273p.
Income from brokerage - including group share of associated undertakings -
rose to Pounds 61m (Pounds 53.4m), offsetting a decline in investment income
to Pounds 5.88m (Pounds 6.63m).
Operating profit rose to Pounds 7.47m (Pounds 5.75m), while income from
interests in associated undertakings improved to Pounds 1.8m (Pounds 1.61m).
Mr Richard Shaw, chairman, pointed to strong growth in new business. In the
UK, for example, income grew by 11 per cent in spite of the recession and
soft insurance markets.
