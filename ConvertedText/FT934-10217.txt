FT  06 NOV 93 / UK Company News: Southnews up 34% and buys nine papers


Southnews, the London regional newspaper publisher, saw a 34 per cent
increase in pre-tax profits for the half year to October 2, up from Pounds
651,000 to Pounds 874,000.
The company is to pay Pounds 3.5m cash for nine regional newspapers
circulating in the M25 region round London. The vendor is Trinity Newspapers
Southern, a subsidiary of Trinity International which is buying the titles
as part of a purchase from Argus Press.
The acquired titles generated revenue of Pounds 6.5m and losses of Pounds
541,000 in the year to March 31.
Turnover on Southnews continuing business was 10 per cent ahead at Pounds
7.16m (Pounds 6.53m) for the half year.
Earnings per share came out 35 per cent ahead at 3.82p (2.84p) and there is
an increased interim of 0.95p (0.7p).
