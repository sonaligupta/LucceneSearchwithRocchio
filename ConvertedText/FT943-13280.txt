FT  20 JUL 94 / UK Company News: Bucknall buoyed by strong second half


   By CLARE GASCOIGNE


Bucknall Group, the construction industry management consultancy, returned
to profit during the year to April 30 with Pounds 236,000 pre-tax, against
losses of Pounds 1.33m.
Turnover rose by 7 per cent from Pounds 15.7m to Pounds 16.8m after a strong
second half.
While overseas operations improved substantially, with first-half operating
profit of Pounds 16,000 rising to Pounds 131,000 for the second six months,
a first-half loss of Pounds 149,000 in UK activities was turned into profits
of Pounds 415,000 in the second half.
There was also a reduction in long-term debt, with net borrowing lowered by
Pounds 368,000, and a fall in net interest payable to Pounds 177,000 (Pounds
223,000).
Gearing dropped from 157 per cent to 122 per cent.
Mr Richard Miles, chairman, said the current year had started well, with a
'significantly higher' order book.
The UK market was showing 'signs of steady improvement,' he added.
Earnings per share were 0.2p compared with losses of 10.5p.
