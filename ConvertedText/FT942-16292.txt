FT  11 APR 94 / Countdown to attack


Sunday morning: Gen Sir Michael Rose, UN commander in Bosnia, abandons trip
to Brussels when told of renewed Serb attack on Gorazde. He says air strikes
are possible.
Afternoon: Serb forces overrun hill close to the centre of Gorazde. Hundreds
of Moslems begin to flee. UNHCR officials say 'all hell has broken lose'.
In Sarajevo, Charles Redman, senior US negotiator in the region, warns that
the Serbs 'will have to be dealt with'.
16.22 GMT: Air strike by two US F-16 aircraft 'in support' of UN forces.
Serbian tanks are thought to be the target.
